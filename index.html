Why use analog systems?

Modern knowledge work is synonymous with digital systems. One way or another, our wrestling with information involves computers. Surprisingly, amid all this digitization, there has been a resurgence of interest in tactile, analog tools to record and share information. Bullet journals, notebooks, fountain pens, and analog watches have all caught our attention, despite their apparent obsolescence. This renewed interest in analog tools and productivity systems goes beyond nostalgia. Non-digital methods of getting knowledge work done are often superior to digital alternatives.
